History of Time

The concept of time has fascinated humans for millennia. Early civilizations used natural events, such as the movement of the sun and moon, to measure time.

Ancient Egyptians developed sundials around 1500 BCE, while Babylonians and Greeks refined timekeeping methods. The invention of mechanical clocks in the 14th century revolutionized time measurement.

In the 20th century, atomic clocks set new standards for accuracy, leading to the creation of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC).

Today, time is measured with extreme precision using atomic and quantum technologies.
